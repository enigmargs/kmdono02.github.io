I am a PhD student in Biostatistics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. With my advisor Dr. Kinh Truong, I work on developing methods extending independent component analysis to analyze functional connectivity and point process data.

Through my work at the Carolina Institute of Developmental Disabilities, I

  • Use machine learning in to predict future autism diagnosis in infants based on behavioral and brain image data
  • Conduct inference on associations between autism diagnosis, behavioral outcomes, and brain measures
  • Teach statistical concepts and programming with R
  • Created a handbook on statistical concepts and R programming

I previously collaborated on the Adolescent Medicine Trials Network for HIV/AIDS Interventions (ATN). My work focused on implementing regression methods with biomarkers subject to limits of detection for analysis on HIV-infected youth with a corresponding R computing package.

For more information, please check out my developed statistical software, publications, CV, and Github account.

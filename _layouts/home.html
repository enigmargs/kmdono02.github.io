I am a PhD student in Biostatistics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I work on developing methods to conduct statistical inference on brain connectivity using independent components analysis.

Through my work at the Carolina Institute of Developmental Disabilities, I:
- Use machine learning to accurately predict future Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) diagnosis in infants based on behavioral and brain image data - Conduct inference on associations between ASD, behavioral outcomes, and brain structure/activity - Teach statistical concepts and programming with R
